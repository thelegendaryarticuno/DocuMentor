DATA HANDLING AND SECURITY POLICY
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: April 1, 2026
Owner: Information Security Office

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines controls for information classification, access management, incident response, and audit readiness across all teams.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third-party vendors with access to company systems or data.

3. LOW PRIORITY GUIDANCE
Low: Teams should keep project folders organized using standard naming conventions to improve document discovery.
Low: Teams should archive non-operational meeting notes every quarter unless legal hold requirements apply.
Low: Teams may use template checklists for routine onboarding tasks to improve consistency.

4. MEDIUM PRIORITY REQUIREMENTS
Medium: Department managers should review access logs weekly and record unresolved anomalies in the compliance tracker.
Medium: Project owners should verify vendor risk questionnaires are current before contract renewal.
Medium: Team leads should confirm backup restoration tests are performed at least once per quarter.

5. HIGH PRIORITY CONTROLS
High: Production credentials must never be shared in chat, email, or ticket comments.
High: Any suspected unauthorized access must be reported to Security Operations within 1 hour.
High: Systems processing customer data must enforce multi-factor authentication and immediate credential rotation after incidents.

6. ENFORCEMENT
Failure to follow this policy may result in disciplinary action, temporary access suspension, mandatory retraining, and formal incident review.
